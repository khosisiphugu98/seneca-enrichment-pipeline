The Enrichment Pipeline

Almost every lead arrives as just a name, an email, and a phone number — sometimes an address. In the US, property ownership is public record, so those three fields are enough to reconstruct who this person is, what property they own, and roughly what a cost-seg study is worth to them — before sales ever picks up the phone. Every price below is verified against the vendor's published pricing (July 2026).

Design rule: a waterfall — cheapest source first, stop at the first confident match, only escalate on a miss. The pipeline proposes; it writes structured fields into GoHighLevel. A human reviews anything customer-facing or money-moving.

2Property lookup~$0.03–0.10 / lead

Address or owner name → the property record County assessor + recorder data

The "deeds office" Dylan mentioned: in the US there's no national deeds registry — each of the 3,144 counties runs its own County Recorder of Deeds (deeds, mortgages, sale prices) and County Assessor (values, tax, characteristics). All public record. Aggregators license and normalize it nationally — ATTOM alone covers recorder data from 2,690+ counties. If Seneca "has access" to one, it's almost certainly PropStream, ATTOM, or DataTree — I'd build on whichever is already paid for.

3Entity + basis~$0.00 (public records)

Find the LLC, estimate the deduction Mostly free public data

Investment property is usually held in an LLC, so an owner-name search on the person can miss the real asset. Person name → state Secretary of State business search (free on every state's website) → LLC names → re-run Stage 2 with the LLC as owner. That's how a Gmail address becomes a $2M apartment building. (Note: OpenCorporates aggregates all states but its commercial API starts ~£2,250/yr — state SoS sites are free, so start there.)

Then the payoff: the deed's last sale price + date is a basis proxy → apply Seneca's own cost-seg math → estimated deduction, pre-computed before first contact. The sales handoff becomes a context card, not a name: "Marcus, 2 LLCs, 3 properties, ~$1.8M combined basis, est. $410k first-year deduction."

GHL inbound webhook fires on new lead → the waterfall runs (a small middleware service or Make/n8n scenario) → results written to GHL custom fields via API. From there it's native GHL: a fit × readiness score routes the lead — high-fit to sales with the context card, mid-fit into the segmented nurture that feeds the existing nurture AI better inputs, junk (VoIP/no-match/no-property) suppressed so nobody wastes a dial. Every estimated deduction is reviewed by a human before it appears in anything a lead sees.
